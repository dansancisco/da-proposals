# Digital Assembly Proposal Structure

## Overview
This document standardizes the content structure for all DA proposals to ensure consistency across different service offerings (Google Ads, SEO, Web Dev, etc.).

---

## Section Breakdown

### 1. **Hero Section** (Universal)
**Location**: Top of page
**Purpose**: Establish the proposal type and set client expectations

**Content:**
- Client name
- Headline: `[Service Type] Proposal` (e.g., "Inbound Engine Proposal", "SEO Growth Proposal", "Website Development Proposal")
- Subheadline: One-sentence value proposition specific to the service
- CTA: "View Investment" (scroll to pricing)

**Badge Components:**
- "Proposal" indicator
- "Prepared for: [Client Name]"
- "Confidential" tag

---

### 2. **Current Situation** (Universal)
**Purpose**: Mirror back the client's pain points and goals from discovery call

**Content Structure:**
- 4-6 bullet points
- First 2-3: Pain points and challenges
- Last 2-3: Goals and desired outcomes
- Written in client's voice (what THEY said, not our interpretation)

**Split Display:**
- Left column: "Pain Points"
- Right column: "Goals & Needs"

**Examples by Channel:**
- **Google Ads**: Plateaued growth, previous agency failures, need predictable pipeline
- **SEO**: Ranking for wrong keywords, competitors outranking them, organic traffic not converting
- **Web Dev**: Outdated site, poor mobile experience, doesn't reflect brand positioning

**Footer Badge**: "Discovery Notes" / "Analysis Status: Confirmed"

---

### 3. **The Solution** (Universal - Channel Customization Required)
**Purpose**: Explain what we're building and which channels we'll use

**Content Structure:**
- **Overview Panel (2/3 width)**: High-level description of the solution (2-3 sentences)
- **Campaigns/Channels Panel (1/3 width)**: List of specific channels/campaigns

**Channel-Specific Campaign Lists:**

**Google Ads:**
- Google Search Ads (+ description of intent targeting)
- Google Local Services Ads (+ Google Guaranteed badge benefit)
- [Optional] Display Remarketing

**SEO:**
- Technical SEO Foundation
- Content Strategy & Creation
- Authority Building (Backlinks)
- [Optional] Local SEO

**Web Dev:**
- Discovery & Strategy
- Design System Development
- Development & Integration
- Launch & Training

**Content Marketing:**
- Content Strategy
- Article Production
- Distribution & Promotion

---

### 4. **Quality Control** (Universal - Adapt by Channel)
**Purpose**: Show the qualification/quality gates for opportunities or work

**Content Structure:**
- 3-5 criteria in grid layout
- Each criterion has:
  - Gate number (01, 02, 03)
  - Title
  - Description
  - Icon

**By Channel:**

**Google Ads / Lead Gen:**
- ICP Match (industry, company size, location)
- Decision Maker (title/authority verification)
- Project Timeline (ready to start within 30-90 days)
- Budget Authority (has budget or can approve)
- Service Area (within defined territory)

**SEO:**
- Keyword Relevance (commercial intent keywords)
- Content Quality (meets E-E-A-T standards)
- Technical Compliance (passes Core Web Vitals)

**Web Dev:**
- Stakeholder Approval (design sign-off gates)
- Browser Compatibility (tested across devices)
- Performance Standards (load time, accessibility)

**Footer Badge**: "Filter Status: 100% Verification" / "Active Filters: X"

---

### 5. **Deliverables** (Universal - Channel Specific)
**Purpose**: Tactical list of what gets delivered

**Content Structure:**
- Two-column checklist
- 8-12 items total
- Organized by phase/priority

**Channel Content:**

**Google Ads:**
- Account setup & campaign build
- Landing page design/development
- Lead qualification workflow
- Call tracking & recording
- Weekly reports
- Monthly strategy calls
- Continuous optimization

**SEO:**
- Technical audit & fixes
- Keyword research & strategy
- Content creation (X articles/month)
- On-page optimization
- Link building (X links/month)
- Monthly performance reports
- Quarterly strategy sessions

**Web Dev:**
- Wireframes & mockups
- Responsive design (mobile/tablet/desktop)
- CMS integration
- Contact forms & integrations
- Performance optimization
- SEO foundation
- Training documentation
- 30-day post-launch support

**Footer Badge**: "Total Deliverables: X Items"

---

### 6. **What's Included** (Universal Features)
**Purpose**: Show standard features that come with every engagement

**Content Structure:**
- 4 cards in grid
- Each with icon, title, description

**Universal Features (Adapt Wording by Channel):**
1. **Direct Communication**
   - Ads: "Live Call Transfers" - Qualified leads transferred directly
   - SEO: "Priority Support" - Direct Slack/email access to strategist
   - Web Dev: "Unlimited Revisions" - Design iterations until satisfied

2. **Quality Assurance**
   - Ads: "Lead Qualification" - Every lead vetted before handoff
   - SEO: "Content Approval" - Review all content before publishing
   - Web Dev: "QA Testing" - Cross-browser/device testing

3. **Transparency**
   - All: "Transparent Reporting" - Real-time dashboard access
   - Specific metrics by channel

4. **Partnership**
   - All: "Dedicated Support" - Direct access to account strategist

---

### 7. **Lead/Content Insights** (Channel Dependent)
**Purpose**: Show what information/assets client receives

**When to Include:**
- **Google Ads**: Always (show lead data format)
- **SEO**: Sometimes (show content deliverable format)
- **Web Dev**: Rarely (show asset delivery format)

**Content Structure:**
- Title: "What You'll See For Each [Lead/Article/Asset]"
- 5-7 bullet points of data fields/components

**Google Ads Lead Example:**
- Business name and contact
- Property type/project scope
- Specific needs
- Timeline/urgency
- Call recording
- Lead score

**SEO Content Example:**
- Target keyword & search volume
- Outline/structure
- Word count & readability score
- Internal/external links added
- Meta data optimized
- Performance tracking setup

---

### 8. **Client Rules** (Universal - Adapt by Channel)
**Purpose**: Set expectations for what CLIENT must do for success

**Content Structure:**
- 3 rules in card grid
- Title + Description for each

**Universal Rules (Adapt Language):**

**Response Time:**
- Ads: "Same-Day Follow-Up" - Contact leads within 4 hours
- SEO: "Timely Approvals" - Review content within 48 hours
- Web Dev: "Quick Feedback" - Respond to design questions within 2 business days

**Feedback Loop:**
- Ads: "Track Every Outcome" - Log lead results in shared system
- SEO: "Share Insights" - Tell us which content resonates with customers
- Web Dev: "Consolidate Feedback" - One point of contact for feedback

**Collaboration:**
- All: "Monthly Reviews" - Join strategy calls to align on goals

---

### 9. **Case Studies** (Universal - Industry Matched)
**Purpose**: Social proof from similar clients

**Content Structure:**
- 2-3 case study cards
- Each includes:
  - Company name (can be anonymized: "Commercial Security Co.")
  - Sector/industry
  - Key metric (headline number)
  - Brief description (1-2 sentences)

**Content Guidelines:**
- Match industry/vertical when possible
- Show outcome metrics relevant to channel:
  - Ads: Qualified opportunities, cost per lead, conversion rate
  - SEO: Organic traffic increase, keyword rankings, leads from organic
  - Web Dev: Conversion rate lift, engagement metrics, business impact

**Image Treatment:**
- Grayscale by default, color on hover
- Use placeholder/stock images or client logos if available

---

### 10. **Testimonials** (Universal - Optional)
**Purpose**: Additional social proof, builds trust

**When to Include:**
- Include if we have 3+ relevant testimonials for this service type
- Skip if we don't have industry-specific testimonials

**Content Structure:**
- Scrolling marquee with 4+ testimonial cards
- Each card:
  - Quote (2-3 sentences max)
  - Name
  - Company
  - Badge (industry/service type)
  - "Verified" indicator

**Badge Categories:**
- Industry specific: "Security Systems", "SaaS", "Professional Services"
- Service specific: "Google Ads Client", "SEO Client", "Web Dev Client"

---

### 11. **Roadmap** (Universal - Channel Customization)
**Purpose**: Show timeline and phasing of work

**Content Structure:**
- 3-5 phases
- Each phase:
  - Timeline label
  - Phase name
  - Description (2-3 sentences)

**Channel Roadmaps:**

**Google Ads (4 phases):**
1. Week 1-2: Discovery & Setup
2. Week 3-4: Campaign Build
3. Week 5-8: Launch & Optimize
4. Month 3+: Scale & Refine

**SEO (4-5 phases):**
1. Month 1: Technical Foundation
2. Month 2-3: Content Production
3. Month 4-6: Authority Building
4. Month 6+: Scale & Maintain

**Web Dev (5 phases):**
1. Week 1-2: Discovery & Strategy
2. Week 3-4: Design & Iteration
3. Week 5-6: Development
4. Week 7-8: Testing & QA
5. Week 9: Launch & Training

**Footer Badge**: "Timeline: On Track" (green indicator)

### 12. **ROI Calculator** (Universal - Default Values by Channel)
**Purpose**: Interactive calculator showing potential return

**Content Structure:**
- Left panel: Input sliders
  - Sales-ready opportunities (or traffic, or conversions)
  - Client LTV
  - Close rate
  - Monthly investment (locked to proposal amount)
- Right panel: Calculated outputs
  - Estimated new clients
  - Revenue generated (monthly)
  - Annual impact
  - Net value
  - ROI percentage
  - Break-even analysis

**Default Values by Channel:**

**Google Ads:**
- Opportunities: 20
- Close Rate: 30%
- LTV: $5,000-$15,000 (depends on service)
- Investment: Proposal amount

**SEO:**
- Monthly Visitors: 500
- Conversion Rate: 3%
- LTV: $5,000-$15,000
- Investment: Proposal amount

**Web Dev:**
- Monthly Visitors: Current traffic
- Conversion Rate Lift: 2x (from 1.5% to 3%)
- LTV: $5,000
- Investment: One-time cost amortized over 12 months

- Each phase shows:

### 14. **Guarantee** (Universal - Customize by Channel)
**Purpose**: Remove risk and show confidence

**Content Structure:**
- Main guarantee statement (1-2 sentences)
- 3-4 stipulations in smaller text

**Guarantees by Channel:**

**Google Ads:**
- **Guarantee**: "If we don't deliver at least [X] qualified opportunities in the first 90 days, we'll manage your campaigns for free until we do."
- **Stipulations**:
  - Minimum 90-day commitment required
  - Ad spend maintained at recommended levels
  - Client must follow up within 4 hours
  - Lead outcomes tracked in shared system

**SEO:**
- **Guarantee**: "If we don't achieve measurable ranking improvements in your target keywords within 90 days, we'll work for free until we do."
- **Stipulations**:
  - Minimum 6-month commitment required
  - Content published on schedule
  - Technical recommendations implemented
  - No prior Google penalties

**Web Dev:**
- **Guarantee**: "If you're not satisfied with the final design, we'll revise it until you are. Unlimited design revisions during the design phase."
- **Stipulations**:
  - Timely feedback provided (within 2 business days)
  - Scope changes require separate agreement
  - Launch timeline depends on content/asset delivery

---

### 15. **Scope Definition** (Universal)
**Purpose**: Clear boundaries of what IS and ISN'T included

**Content Structure:**
- Two columns: "Included" | "Not Included"
- 6-10 items per column
- Uses checkmarks (green) and X marks (red)

**Scope by Channel:**

**Google Ads:**
- **Included**: Search ads, landing page, lead qualification, call tracking, reporting, optimization
- **Excluded**: Organic SEO, social ads, website development, CRM setup, sales coaching

**SEO:**
- **Included**: Technical SEO, content creation, on-page optimization, link building, reporting
- **Excluded**: Paid ads, social media, website redesign, PR/outreach, local citations (unless specified)

**Web Dev:**
- **Included**: Design, development, CMS integration, mobile responsive, SEO foundation, training
- **Excluded**: Logo design, copywriting, photography, ongoing maintenance (unless added), e-commerce functionality (unless specified)

**Content Marketing:**
- **Included**: Strategy, content creation, editing, SEO optimization, basic distribution
- **Excluded**: Paid promotion, social media management, video production, graphic design

**Content Structure:**

**Universal FAQs (Include Across All Channels):**

**Channel-Specific FAQs:**

## Content Tone & Voice Guidelines

### Writing Principles
1. **Client-Centric**: Write from client's perspective, not ours
2. **Specific > Generic**: Use concrete numbers and examples
3. **Benefits > Features**: Show outcomes, not just activities
4. **Direct & Clear**: No jargon or marketing fluff
5. **Confident**: We know what we're doing, no hedging

### Tone by Section
- **Situation**: Empathetic, understanding ("we hear you")
- **Solution**: Confident, strategic ("here's how we fix it")
- **Deliverables**: Concrete, tactical ("this is what you get")
- **Investment**: Transparent, justified ("here's why it costs this")
- **Guarantee**: Bold, risk-reversing ("we've got your back")

## Visual Hierarchy Standards

### Section Headers
- **Title**: Font Display, 4xl, Bold, Uppercase
- **Subtitle**: Font Mono, sm, Uppercase, text-neutral-500
- **Badge**: Optional top-right indicator (signal color dot + text)

### Content Blocks
- **Primary Cards**: White background, black border, hover:bg-neutral-50
- **Highlight Cards**: Black background, signal accents, white text
- **Grid Layouts**: gap-px, border-black, bg-black (creates 1px dividers)

### Colors
- **Primary**: Black (#000000)
- **Signal**: Orange (#ff4d00)
- **Background**: White (#ffffff)
- **Grid**: Subtle grid pattern (bg-grid-tech)
- **Text**: Black primary, neutral-500/600 secondary

**Include All Sections Except:**

**Include All Sections Except:**

**Include All Sections Except:**
